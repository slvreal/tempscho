Ada Lovelace

Biography

Ada Lovelace was born in London on December 10, 1815. Her mother was Anne Isabella Milbanke, and her father was the poet George Gordon Byron. Her parents separated when she was very young; raised by her mother, she received a strong education in mathematics in the hope that Ada would grow up very different from her poet father. As a teen she befriended mathematician and inventor Charles Babbage, through whom she was able to begin studying advanced mathematics with a University of London professor. Ada married in 1835 and had three children. She died on November 27, 1852.

Contributions to Computer Science

Ada’s contributions are best understood in the context of her friendship with Charles Babbage. After seeing his notes and his prototype for a “difference engine,” she became fascinated with an “analytical engine.”

When asked to translate an article about the Analytical Engine, Ada produced a translation and added extensive notes of her own. Unlike Babbage, who mainly envisioned the machine as performing calculations, Ada described how it could follow an algorithm. In these notes she included an example that many consider the first computer program, nearly 100 years before a working computer was built.

List of important papers/ contributions

Translation of “Sketch of the Analytical Engine Invented by Charles Babbage” (1842),
with notes. Algorithm for computing Bernoulli numbers. Vision of a programmable computing machine.
